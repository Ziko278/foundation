WHO WE ARE

VINCENT AND ANNE FOUNDATION is a non- governmental, not-for-profit, core philanthropic and advocacy organisation.

The plight of the less privileged stemmed our decision to come together and organize a body that will constantly and concertedly proffer assistance to those that are in dire living condition. Our focus is on protecting and providing for the helpless, the needy, the disadvantaged and the displaced in society. We do well to revive the hope of the hopeless by giving to their needs, and organizing programs that ensure self reliance, we also speak on their behalf, advocating help for them.

With the aid of modern technology like the internet, we see the world as a global unit that can be toured in a very short span of time, so we leverage on this to have our presence felt everywhere.

In driving our goal, we partner with

  • Public spirited individuals
  • Bigger not-for-profit organisations
  • Corporate entities
  • Religious organisations.

    • VINCENT AND ANNE FOUNDATION devotes effort, resources and time for the welfare of humanity. We don't just do charitable acts and volunteer work, we follow up to see that the charity is creating positive impacts. We work with people and we are positively touching lives. Humanity's welfare is our goal

OUR IDEOLOGY

Our Mission

We develop, promote and encourage people participation in charitable work. We build support by acting both on the problem and providing succour in a digital way, leveraging on this internet and network age.

Our Vision

To see a society where the care and love for humanity is paramount and see help reaching the helpless in time, anywhere they are.

Our Focus

We focus on the HELPLESS.

  • We assist the displaced

WE CAN DO BETTER

There was a circulated photo of a vulture waiting for a starving child to die so it could feast on her corps. The photo was taken by Kevin Carter, a South African photo journalist who later won the Pulitzers prize for this amazing shot (edited and left out here because of its inhumane posture). Kevin however lived just a few months to enjoy his supposed achievement because he later got depressed and took his own life. He was savouring his feat and was being celebrated on major news channels and networks worldwide for such an exceptional photographic skill. His depression started when, during one of the interviews he granted (a phone-in program) someone phoned in and asked him what later happened to the child. Kevin replied, ‘I didn’t wait to find out after this shot as a I had a flight to catch and the person replied. ‘I put it to you that there were two vultures on that day: one had a camera. His constant thought of that statement led to depression and his ultimate suicide. In whatever we do we should let humanity come first before what we can gain out of the situation. Kevin Carter could have been alive today and even be more celebrated if he had done that little act of kindness by picking that little child up and taken to the United Nations feeding centre where she was attempting to reach. In all we do, let us always think of others and how we can be of benefit to humanity. How we can lend a helping hand and wipe away tears in whatever blessed position that we are, let us think of how we can use it to benefit the people most.
